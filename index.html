Smoking the Perfect Prime Rib

A simple guide to making a tender, flavorful prime rib on a smoker.


First of all,

You need to know why you would want to smoke a prime rib.

Red meat, especially beef, has long been valued for its rich flavor, high protein content, and important nutrients such as iron, zinc, and vitamin B12. Among all the cuts of beef, prime rib stands out as one of the best parts of the cow because of its exceptional tenderness and marbling. Taken from the rib section, prime rib contains a perfect balance of meat and fat, which allows it to stay juicy and develop deep flavor when cooked properly. This marbling melts during cooking, enhancing both texture and taste in a way that leaner cuts cannot match. When prepared correctly, prime rib represents the peak of what beef has to offer, combining richness, tenderness, and bold flavor into a single, memorable cut.

Why Smoke a Prime Rib?

Smoking prime rib adds a deep, rich flavor while keeping the meat incredibly juicy. With the right preparation and temperature control, you can achieve steakhouse-quality results at home.


Doesn't that look delicious?

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Preparation
    • Choose a well-marbled prime rib
    • Season generously with salt and pepper
    • Let it rest at room temperature
  2. Smoking
    • Set smoker to 225°F
    • Use oak or hickory wood
    • Smoke until internal temp reaches 120–125°F
  3. Finishing
    • Remove from smoker
    • Rest for 20 minutes
    • Sear at high heat if desired
